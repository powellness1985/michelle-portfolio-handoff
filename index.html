Positioning idea

Consider leading with the clearest booking identity.

Michelle Krueger
Commercial Actor & Print Talent | English/Spanish | Austin / LA-ready

The current site shows several real strengths at once: actor, print talent, photographer, videographer, editor, and producer. Those can all stay part of the story, but one possible improvement is to help a busy visitor answer one question quickly: “What could I book Michelle for?”

A practical direction could be to lead with commercial acting, print, lifestyle, spokesperson, and bilingual English/Spanish work.

Suggestions

Seven optional updates that could help the site work harder.

01

Make “Work” the homepage.

Consider renaming “Work” to “Home” and making it a true landing page. Because the current homepage leads with a Post-Production/Editor Reel, a new visitor may first read the site as an editing portfolio instead of an on-camera talent portfolio.

02

Make the reel easy to find.

Put the strongest reel near the top of the homepage and keep a dedicated Reels page. Backstage recommends strong, short clips and getting to the best material quickly because casting may only watch the first clip or two.

03

Show brand names as proof.

Commercial credits like American Express, KFC, Vivo, Zenu, Diners Club, Esan, Galletas Artesanales La Victoria, and Alicorp Ali could be visible before someone clicks a video.

04

Add captions to Print.

The print images are useful campaign proof, but each one needs context: brand, campaign, media type, and role.

05

Move Photography out of the main path.

Product, maternity, children, interiors, and family photography show range, but they may pull attention away from the booking message. One option is to keep them separate as “Behind the Camera” or “Photography Services.”

06

Add Resume and Contact pages.

Actor/talent sites often make headshot, reel, resume, and contact easy to find. A Resume and Contact page could include location, languages, union status, representation if applicable, and a simple contact path.

07

Update SEO titles and descriptions.

Use simple page titles like “Michelle Krueger | Commercial Actor & Print Talent,” “Commercial Reel | Michelle Krueger,” and “Contact | Michelle Krueger.”

Best bang for the buck

Do free improvements first, then pay only when ready.

  1. Rewrite the homepage around booking Michelle as commercial actor and print talent.
  2. Add captions to Commercials and Print.
  3. Add Resume and Contact pages.
  4. Move or rename Photography so it does not distract from booking.
  5. Add SEO titles and descriptions.
  6. Check mobile view before sharing the site widely.

The first paid upgrade worth considering is the lowest Wix plan that removes Wix branding and allows a custom domain. Fixing structure and content will create most of the improvement; a custom-coded website or developer maintenance plan is not necessary right now.

I need help updating my existing Wix portfolio site for Michelle Krueger.

Current site:
https://michikru.wixsite.com/michellekrueger

Goal:
Attract commercial acting, print, lifestyle, spokesperson, and bilingual English/Spanish booking opportunities.

Audience:
Casting directors, agents, commercial producers, ad agencies, brands, and creative directors.

Recommended positioning:
Michelle Krueger
Commercial Actor & Print Talent | English/Spanish | Austin / LA-ready

Recommended navigation:
Home
Reels
Commercials
Print
About
Resume
Contact

Please help me create:
1. Homepage section-by-section copy
2. A rewritten About page
3. Captions for commercial work
4. Captions for print work
5. A Resume page checklist
6. A Contact page structure
7. SEO page titles and meta descriptions
8. A step-by-step Wix editing checklist

Important:
- Keep the tone polished, warm, professional, and booking-focused.
- Do not make up credits, union status, representation, height, hair, eyes, years, or exact roles.
- Use placeholders where facts need to be confirmed.
- Keep the photography/video/editing background, but do not let it distract from commercial acting and print work.
- Make this easy for a non-technical person to implement in Wix.

Final thought

The site does not need to be complicated.

It needs to answer quickly:

  1. Who is Michelle?
  2. What should someone book her for?
  3. What proof does she have?
  4. How can they watch her reel?
  5. How can they contact her?

Answering those questions a little faster could make the site feel more immediately bookable.
